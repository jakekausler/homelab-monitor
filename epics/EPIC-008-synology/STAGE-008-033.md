## Overview

`builtin/host.py`'s `HostCollector` probes its configured `extra_mountpoints` (default `["/rackstation"]`) via `psutil.disk_usage(mountpoint)` â€” a BLOCKING call with NO timeout. On a wedged hard-NFS mount this HANGS the host collector: the scheduler's per-collector timeout records a "timeout" failure, but the underlying blocking psutil thread leaks (it stays blocked until the NFS export recovers). This is a real latent bug discovered during STAGE-008-018 (which added the timeout-safe liveness probe under a SEPARATE metric `homelab_synology_mount_up`, but deliberately left host.py untouched as out-of-scope).

Harden `host.py` so its blocking filesystem probes are non-hanging, mirroring the pattern STAGE-008-018 established in `synology_mount_health.py`: a `/proc/self/mountinfo` presence-check (cheap, never hangs) before probing, then the blocking `psutil.disk_usage` run in a BOUNDED dedicated `ThreadPoolExecutor` wrapped in an `asyncio.wait_for` timeout, so a wedged hard mount is reported (or skipped) within the timeout instead of leaking unbounded threads.

**Vertical slice:** after this stage, a wedged `/rackstation` (or any `extra_mountpoints`) NFS mount no longer hangs `HostCollector` or leaks threads â€” the disk-usage probe times out gracefully (the mountpoint is skipped / its bytes omitted) and the collector continues.

## Concrete deliverables

### 1. Non-hanging disk-usage probe in host.py

Wrap `HostCollector`'s `psutil.disk_usage(mountpoint)` (the `extra_mountpoints` probe in `_collect_disk_usage`) so a wedged hard-NFS mount cannot hang the collector or leak unbounded threads: a `/proc/self/mountinfo` presence-check first (skip a mount that's not present), then the blocking `psutil.disk_usage` in a BOUNDED dedicated `ThreadPoolExecutor` under an `asyncio.wait_for(timeout=...)`. On timeout/OSError: skip the mountpoint (omit its `homelab_host_disk_bytes`) + record the error, and continue. Mirror `synology_mount_health.py`'s helpers/shape. Consider whether the `psutil.disk_partitions` loop's per-partition `disk_usage` calls need the same treatment (the partition loop also calls disk_usage â€” scope: at minimum the `extra_mountpoints` NFS probe; evaluate the partition loop too).
