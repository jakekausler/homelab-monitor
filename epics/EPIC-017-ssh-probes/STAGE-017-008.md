**No-silent-deferral assessment:** The deferred cron-SSH is a CONDITIONAL capability with no committed trigger ("build if/when a remote host with real custom crons appears"). The workflow's no-silent-deferral rule targets SILENT drops; here the deferral is loudly + fully documented in TWO authoritative epic docs (EPIC-017.md already; EPIC-002.md via this stage) with the architecture, the dependency (017's transport), the trigger condition, and the user-facing consequence all recorded. That IS the appropriate tracked form for an uncommitted future capability — a phantom stage/epic would manufacture noise. If/when committed, it lands as a write-path stage in a FUTURE epic (not under 017 — complete + read-only — nor 002 — heartbeat/cron-state).
