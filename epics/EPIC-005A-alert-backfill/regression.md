- `deploy/vmalert/metrics/log_drain_health.yaml` loads on the live vmalert-metrics with all 5 rules `health=ok`, empty `lastError` (CardinalityBudgetExceeded, DrainStall [critical], DrainNoProgress, MetricFamilyDroppedSeries, ServiceLogVolumeSpike). The `on()` modifier is LOAD-BEARING: a bare `and sum(...)` is a VictoriaMetrics-only empty-label broadcast that promtool CANNOT evaluate (promtool's `and` needs label-set match; `sum()` yields `{}`). `and on() (...)` works in BOTH engines and keeps the LEFT operand's labels. - DRAIN CADENCE: the drain runs on a FIXED 5-minute interval (`interval_seconds=300`). `increase(homelab_drain_cycle_duration_seconds_count[20m])` ≈ 4 at baseline. The stall window is `[20m]` (4 cycles of headroom) — do NOT shrink to `[15m]` (only 3 cycles → false-fires on a single slow/skipped cycle after a restart). The `for: 5m` means a real stall fires after ~25min of silence = 5× the interval.
- ServiceLogVolumeSpike `sum by (service_key)(sum_over_time(homelab_log_signature_count[5m])) > clamp_min(4 * avg_over_time((...)[7d:5m]), 50)` (live-replay-only — the `[7d:5m]` subquery returns got:[] under promtool). `homelab_log_signature_count` is a per-cycle GAUGE (use `sum_over_time`, NOT rate — `_total` is also a gauge, not a counter). K=4× per-service (smoother than per-signature so a tighter multiplier than the renderer's 5×).
- REAL-FIRE AT REFINEMENT (correct behavior, documented): on the prod rig ServiceLogVolumeSpike fired for `homelab-grafana` (~2420/5m vs 7d-avg 110, ~22× threshold) and `homelab-vmagent` (~400 vs 28, ~3.6×). Cross-check: homelab-monitor (high-volume but steady) is at 1.24× its baseline → correctly NOT firing. The rule is working as designed (detecting a real anomaly). Regression note: when re-validating, a firing ServiceLogVolumeSpike is only a false-fire if the service's CURRENT 5m volume is CLOSE to its 1h/3h baseline but the 7d-avg is artificially low (true cold-start) — compare 7d-vs-1h baselines to distinguish.
- No double-fire with existing rules: ServiceErrorRateSpike (error-rate per container, different metric/axis), NewLogSignature (`homelab_log_signature_new==1`, new pattern), SignatureWentSilent (`homelab_log_signature_silent==1`) — all distinct from per-service total volume. Labels on all 5 rules: source_tool: vmalert-metrics, category: log-anomaly, + per-rule anomaly_kind + target_kind. The LogsQL absolute-threshold spike form was REJECTED in Design (noisy across order-of-magnitude-different per-service baselines); no `deploy/vmalert/logs/log_anomalies.yaml` was created.
