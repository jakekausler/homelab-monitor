- `deploy/vmalert/metrics/cron_duration.yaml` (RULE 1 `CronRunDurationAnomalous`) and `deploy/vmalert/metrics/cron_duration_cadence.yaml` (RULE 2 `CronRunDurationNearCadence`) both load on the live vmalert-metrics with `health=ok`, empty `lastError`, state inactive, and do NOT false-fire at the real baseline. - RULE 1 rolling-baseline `homelab_heartbeat_last_duration_seconds{wrapper_installed="yes"} > clamp_min(3 * avg_over_time(same[1d]), 120)`: the floor=120s is the load-bearing anti-false-fire guard — it keeps rtlamr-watchdog.sh's natural 74s max silent (74 < 120). `homelab_heartbeat_last_duration_seconds` is a PERSISTED LAST-VALUE GAUGE (re-emitted every 30s until the next run) so for the daily job avg_over_time[1d] ≈ the current value (near self-comparison); the rule mainly fires on a fresh in-window spike or sustained degradation. Live-replay: fires at 500 (baseline 100), silent at flat 100.
- RULE 2 cadence-relative `homelab_heartbeat_last_duration_seconds{wrapper_installed="yes"} > 0.5 * label_value(same, "cadence_seconds")`: live-replay proved fire backup@50000 (>43200), silent rtlamr@30 and @74 (<150 — no false-fire on natural variance), fire rtlamr@200 (>150 — catches a genuine hang). This is the robust hang detector (works identically for the stable daily job and the variable 5-min job). Series WITHOUT a `cadence_seconds` label correctly return NaN from label_value() and are excluded.
- No overlap with existing heartbeat rules: the 7 `homelab.heartbeats` rules fire on staleness/overdue/failure/flapping; these 2 fire on run-DURATION while the job still succeeds. Labels: severity warning, source_tool: vmalert-metrics, target_kind: cron, routing_channel: cron-health (matching the cron family). Too-fast rule DROPPED (Design finding — noise on rtlamr's 0.16× natural fast exits).

## STAGE-005A-006 — Logging + Drain anomaly rules

- `deploy/vmalert/metrics/log_drain_health.yaml` loads on the live vmalert-metrics with all 5 rules `health=ok`, empty `lastError` (CardinalityBudgetExceeded, DrainStall [critical], DrainNoProgress, MetricFamilyDroppedSeries, ServiceLogVolumeSpike). Regression check: `/api/v1/rules` shows the 5 rules health=ok. 4 of 5 are silent at the real baseline (cardinality_warn=0, drain healthy, dropped_series all 0). ServiceLogVolumeSpike fires only on a genuine sustained per-service volume step-change (see below) — NOT a baseline false-fire.
- DrainStall + DrainNoProgress use the gate `and on() (sum(homelab_log_stream_lines_per_sec) > 1)`. The `on()` modifier is LOAD-BEARING: a bare `and sum(...)` is a VictoriaMetrics-only empty-label broadcast that promtool CANNOT evaluate (promtool's `and` needs label-set match; `sum()` yields `{}`). `and on() (...)` works in BOTH engines and keeps the LEFT operand's labels. Regression check: `promtool test rules __tests__/log_drain_health.tests.yaml` → SUCCESS (RULES 1-4); live-replay confirmed DrainStall fires on a planted flat-count stall + is silent when the count rises, and DrainNoProgress is mutually exclusive with DrainStall (its `increase(count[20m]) > 0` guard). If a future edit reverts the gate to bare `and sum(...)`, RULES 2/3 silently stop firing under promtool AND lose label propagation.
- DRAIN CADENCE: the drain runs on a FIXED 5-minute interval (`interval_seconds=300`). `increase(homelab_drain_cycle_duration_seconds_count[20m])` ≈ 4 at baseline. The stall window is `[20m]` (4 cycles of headroom) — do NOT shrink to `[15m]` (only 3 cycles → false-fires on a single slow/skipped cycle after a restart). The `for: 5m` means a real stall fires after ~25min of silence = 5× the interval.
- ServiceLogVolumeSpike `sum by (service_key)(sum_over_time(homelab_log_signature_count[5m])) > clamp_min(4 * avg_over_time((...)[7d:5m]), 50)` (live-replay-only — the `[7d:5m]` subquery returns got:[] under promtool). `homelab_log_signature_count` is a per-cycle GAUGE (use `sum_over_time`, NOT rate — `_total` is also a gauge, not a counter). Live-replay: fires for a service summing 300/5m vs a 7d-avg ~21 (threshold ~86); a quiet service spiking to 40 stays silent (floor=50 blocks it). floor=50 is the anti-quiet-service + cold-start guard. K=4× per-service (smoother than per-signature so a tighter multiplier than the renderer's 5×).
- REAL-FIRE AT REFINEMENT (correct behavior, documented): on the prod rig ServiceLogVolumeSpike fired for `homelab-grafana` (~2420/5m vs 7d-avg 110, ~22× threshold) and `homelab-vmagent` (~400 vs 28, ~3.6×). Verified NOT a false-fire / NOT cold-start: `homelab_log_signature_count` has full 7-day history; both services stepped up ~20-30× SIMULTANEOUSLY at ~14:47 UTC (correlating with the monitor restart from STAGE-005A-004's probe work) and held the new level — a genuine sustained behavioral change from a stable 7-day baseline. Cross-check: homelab-monitor (high-volume but steady) is at 1.24× its baseline → correctly NOT firing. The rule is working as designed (detecting a real anomaly). Regression note: when re-validating, a firing ServiceLogVolumeSpike is only a false-fire if the service's CURRENT 5m volume is CLOSE to its 1h/3h baseline but the 7d-avg is artificially low (true cold-start) — compare 7d-vs-1h baselines to distinguish.
- No double-fire with existing rules: ServiceErrorRateSpike (error-rate per container, different metric/axis), NewLogSignature (`homelab_log_signature_new==1`, new pattern), SignatureWentSilent (`homelab_log_signature_silent==1`) — all distinct from per-service total volume. Labels on all 5 rules: source_tool: vmalert-metrics, category: log-anomaly, + per-rule anomaly_kind + target_kind. The LogsQL absolute-threshold spike form was REJECTED in Design (noisy across order-of-magnitude-different per-service baselines); no `deploy/vmalert/logs/log_anomalies.yaml` was created.
